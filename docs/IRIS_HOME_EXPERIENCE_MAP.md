## Scene Breakdown

# Scene 01 — Opening Sequence

## Purpose

Establish the perceived value of IRIS before explaining the brand.

The visitor should immediately feel that this is a premium creative brand.

Before the visitor knows what IRIS does, they should already believe that IRIS is different.

---

## Experience

The opening lasts between 2 and 4 seconds.

The logo appears with confidence.

The motion is calm.

The atmosphere is minimal.

Nothing competes for attention.

There is no loading spinner.

There is no progress bar.

The opening feels intentional rather than technical.

It should feel like the beginning of a premium short film, not a website loader.

---

## Visual Direction

Use a minimal premium visual language.

Background should be soft, clean, and cinematic.

Preferred feeling:

* Cream
* Deep plum
* Soft light
* Subtle grain
* Gentle depth
* No visual noise

The IRIS logo should be the only dominant element.

Do not add decorative elements unless they support the feeling of premium value.

---

## Motion Direction

Motion must be slow, elegant, and controlled.

Recommended motion:

* Logo fade in
* Slight scale from 0.96 to 1
* Soft reveal
* Gentle blur removal
* Fade transition into Scene 02

Forbidden motion:

* Bounce
* Spin
* Flash
* Shake
* Fast movement
* Loading spinner animation

---

## Assets Required

* IRIS logo
* Temporary logo animation placeholder
* Background texture or subtle gradient
* Soft lighting overlay
* Optional ambient audio placeholder

All missing assets should be represented as placeholders.

Do not block implementation because assets are missing.

---

## Success Criteria

Scene 01 succeeds only if the visitor thinks:

"This brand feels premium."

before asking:

"What do they actually do?"

If the opening feels like a technical loader, it fails.

If the opening feels cheap, noisy, or generic, it fails.

If the opening can be removed without weakening the first impression, it fails.
